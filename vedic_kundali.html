🔮 Past Life & Karmic Indicators
☋ Ketu in Cancer (2nd House) — What You Carry From Past Lives

Ketu in Cancer in the 2nd house speaks of a soul that has lived many lifetimes in roles of deep nurturing, family care, and emotional service. You were a caretaker — a mother, healer, or provider of emotional sustenance — so naturally skilled in these areas that they now feel hollow or "already done."

You may notice a natural detachment from money and material security — not because you don't deserve it, but because your soul knows these are impermanent. Speech and voice may carry an unusual quality — sometimes blunt, sometimes profound. Family karma (particularly around the father's lineage or wealth) requires healing in this lifetime.

Past-life gifts brought into this birth: emotional intelligence, psychic sensitivity, intuitive cooking/healing/nurturing abilities, deep memory, and connection to ancestral wisdom. These feel effortless to you.

✦ · · · ✦
☊ Rahu in Capricorn (8th House) — Your Karmic Destiny

Rahu's placement in Capricorn (8th house) is a powerful directive from the cosmos: this lifetime, you are meant to master the hidden, the transformative, and the structured aspects of the material and spiritual worlds. Where Ketu brings past gifts of nurturing and emotion, Rahu pulls you toward ambition, discipline, research, and power.

The 8th house is the house of tantra, astrology, occult sciences, surgery, research, inheritances, and sudden transformations. Rahu here gives a deep, almost magnetic pull toward mysticism and the unknown. Many great astrologers, healers, researchers, and transformational figures have Rahu in the 8th.

Karmic mission: Move from the comfort of emotional security (Cancer/Ketu) into the mastery of systems, structures, and hidden knowledge (Capricorn/Rahu). The soul is learning to combine intuition with discipline.

✦ · · · ✦
♄ Saturn + Moon in 7th House — Relationship Karma

The Moon-Saturn conjunction in the 7th house of partnerships is a profound karmic signature. In past lives, you made vows, contracts, or deep commitments that were not fully honored — or you were bound in relationships through duty rather than love. Saturn now teaches you that true partnership must combine both: commitment AND love.

This placement often indicates a delay in meaningful relationships, or attracting partners who are older, more serious, or carry heavy responsibilities. However, once you find your true partner (often after Saturn matures around age 29-32), the relationship becomes a pillar of extraordinary strength and depth.

The 7th house is also about open enemies and public image. Saturn here gifts a quiet authority — people respect you, perhaps even fear you slightly, because you carry discipline and wisdom in your public interactions.
